Project Brief

The sweetest fast food brand you never expected.

Fanny Flan sits at the intersection of savory fast food and sweet personality. The brand tension is the entire brief: serious food quality, absolutely not serious about itself.

The target customer craves smash burgers and flan in the same visit. They are not confused. They are exactly right. The brand identity had to hold both ends without collapsing into a pun.

Scope covered wordmark explorations, full packaging system, social media content, people and lifestyle imagery, and apparel. All generated through AI with direction at every step.

Design Decisions

Four calls that make it work.

The brand has to be warm, bold, and slightly irreverent. Every decision was tested against that read.

01

Warm yellow on near-black, rust as the punch

#F5C842 primary, #C4622D as accent. Yellow reads as fun and appetite-stimulating without going fast-food cliche. The rust accent gives it heat and edge. Together they read as premium casual: not cheap, not precious.

02

Script-adjacent wordmark with star punctuation

Fanny Flan uses a personality-forward wordmark that reads as handwritten confidence, not amateur. The star symbol (✦) appears throughout as a brand tic: in slogans, labels, and social posts. It signals quality without being formal.

03

Dual tagline system

"Fast food. Sweet personality." is the brand statement. "Savory bites. Sweet vibes." is the product promise. Both run independently. Both work on packaging, social, and apparel. The rule: one tagline per surface, never both together.

04

People-first brand photography

The people category was intentional from the start. Fanny Flan is not a product brand, it is a personality brand. Customers are characters. The lifestyle imagery was designed to feel like user-generated content at brand quality, not polished studio photography.
