Project Brief

A challenger brand built for Gen Z from scratch.

Design an energy drink that could compete with Monster and Ghost on shelf, on social, and in a pitch deck. Constraint: every visual asset had to come from AI generation, no Illustrator, no Photoshop, no Figma.

Target: 18 to 28, gym-adjacent, drawn to brands with attitude. Emotional territory: controlled chaos. Ambitious, loud, confident.

Full scope: name, wordmark direction, can label design across four variants, texture system, and a social campaign with 30+ assets in five categories.

Design Decisions

Four calls that define the brand.

Each decision was made for a specific visual and commercial reason, not aesthetic preference.

01

Acid lime on near-black

#CAFF4C on #050d01. Not standard lime green. An acid, almost yellow-green that reads as high voltage at label scale and from shelf distance. Extreme contrast by design: seen before it is read.

02

Halftone dot texture at 6% opacity

28px dot grid on every can background. Reference: zines, screenprinting, DIY streetwear. Tactile depth without competing with the wordmark. Reads as texture on screen, would feel physical in print.

03

Condensed grotesque, max weight, tight kerning

No border radius. No gradients except in backlit contexts. The wordmark works at 2 inches on a can and 20 feet on an event backdrop. Copy follows the same logic: imperatives, not taglines.

04

Four variants with one shared parent

Flagship lime-on-black. Zero sugar reverse white. B Vitamins warm tint. Clean Energy cool offset. Each variant shifts palette while holding the same label architecture. Same logic as Celsius and Ghost.

The AI Workflow

What actually happened inside the prompts.

01

Brief before prompts

Target, emotional territory, competitive set defined in writing before generating anything. That brief became the filter for every output evaluated afterward.

02

Specificity over imagination

Prompts specified exact hex values, texture density, label hierarchy ratios, and typographic constraints. Generic prompts produce generic outputs.

03

Iteration as creative direction

50+ variations generated. 80% discarded. Refinements targeted specific problems: kerning, texture weight, can proportion, wordmark size relative to claims.

04

System test: five categories

The brand was extended into all five asset categories without manual production. If the system is solid, AI populates new formats without the brand breaking.

AI was the tool.
Direction was everything.

Anyone can open ChatGPT and ask for a can design. Very few know what to ask, how to evaluate the output, when to push further, and when to ship.

This is a demonstration of creative direction applied through AI workflows. A brand that could compete on shelf, in feed, and in a pitch deck. Zero traditional production hours.
